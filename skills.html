Gardening

Buying my first home was the catalyst for my interest in gardening. My interest in improving the appearance and atmosphere of my yard grew into an interest in native plants. From there I added a vegetable garden. Eventually I removed every bit of grass on my property and turned my yard into a native plant and kitchen garden. A desire to improve my growing skills lead to volunteering on a local certified organic farm...which lead to an internship...which eventually lead to quiting my job for a full-time season-long on-farm internship on another farm. That lead to a full-time season-long on-farm apprenticeship the next year.

During these years I also supported other growers by managing a winter farmers' market. I've worked with the herd at an organic dairy. I've farmed with tractors and horses. I helped create and was the first program coordinator for my local food banks' Garden and Gleaning Programs. I've shared my skills and knowledge with other gardeners as the manager of three community gardens.

These days I have a rather elaborate ornamental garden and food forest at my current home. My current home is in a warmer hardiness zone and as a result I've developed a passion for growing dahlias. This fall was my first dividing and storing tubers. I look forward to growing up my stock and perhaps I'll start to dabble in breeding new varieties.

All this is to say that by following my curiosity I went from being a homeowner with a yard with which she didn't know what to do to an avid hobby gardener to eventually developing my skills enough to becoming a professional grower and gardening educator.
